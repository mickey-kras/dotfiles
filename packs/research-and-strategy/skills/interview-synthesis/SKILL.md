# Interview Synthesis

Use this skill to process notes from stakeholder or expert interviews into
structured findings.

Process:
1. read all interview notes before beginning synthesis
2. extract key claims, preferences, and concerns per interviewee
3. group findings by theme across interviewees
4. identify areas of consensus and areas of disagreement
5. note the strength of each finding (single source vs. corroborated)
6. flag emotional signals (urgency, frustration, enthusiasm)
7. produce a synthesis document with themes, evidence, and open questions

Output structure:
- executive summary (3-5 bullet points)
- theme-by-theme findings with supporting quotes or references
- alignment matrix (who agrees on what)
- tension map (where views conflict and why)
- recommended follow-up questions or investigations
